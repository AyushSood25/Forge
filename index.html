    SECTION 05
    Eating Out
    Restaurant survival. Read this before friends call. Knowing what to order beats ordering nothing.
    The food tiers
    Eat freely
    Tandoori paneer · Grilled veg starters · Roomali roti · Steamed momos · Salads · Plain dal · Sabzi without cream · Raita · Egg dishes (if available) · Soya kebabs
    Eat in moderation
    Paneer butter masala (small portion) · Naan (one max) · Biryani (skip the ghee bowl) · Fried momos · Veg manchurian dry · Schezwan rice (half plate) · Pizza (2 slices, thin crust)
    Eat sparingly
    Butter naan · Malai kofta · Cream pasta · Cheese-loaded anything · Desserts · Sweet lassi · Cocktails · Fried street food (samosa, kachori, vada pav)
    Tactics
    The pre-restaurant whey hack
    Drink a whey shake 30 min before leaving home. You'll arrive less hungry and order 30% less without effort.
    Order first
    Place your order before the group settles in. Anchors you to your choice before social pressure shifts it.
    Water before alcohol
    If you drink, alternate: 1 drink, 1 glass of water. Cuts intake by half. And alcohol is 7 cal/g — second only to fat.
    The 24-hour rule
    If you overate last night, don't undereat today. Just eat normally. One day doesn't matter. Four days do.
    Damage control protocol
    After a heavy meal: 20-min walk that night, water first thing next morning, regular gym next day. Do NOT skip workout to "punish" yourself.
    The alcohol truth
    A beer is 150 cal. A cocktail is 250-400 cal. A whiskey neat is 70 cal. Pick neat spirits over beer over cocktails if losing weight.
    The "I already ate" line
    When pressed to eat more, just say "I'm full" or "I ate before coming." No explanation needed. Friends respect "no" without a lecture.

    SECTION 07
    Rules
    The non-negotiables. The ones that survive bad weeks, festivals, and friend pressure.
    ONE
    Track everything for the first 30 days
    HealthifyMe app. Every bite, every shake, every chai. After 30 days you'll know portions intuitively. People who skip this stay surprised when nothing changes.
    TWO
    The 24-hour rule
    If you fall off — bad meal, missed gym, drinks night — you're back on within 24 hours. Not 2 weeks. Not next Monday. Tomorrow morning. This single rule separates people who succeed from those who don't.
    THREE
    Sleep 7+ hours, no exceptions
    Less than this and your fat loss slows by 30%. Cortisol goes up, hunger hormones go haywire. You'll hold belly fat no matter what you eat.
    FOUR
    Weigh once a week, same conditions
    Sunday morning, after bathroom, before eating, in underwear. Daily weighing will mess with your head — water fluctuations are 1-2 kg up or down.
    FIVE
    Gym 4× a week, missed weeks max 2 total
    Over 6 months, you can miss 8 weeks (2 weeks/month). Use them on travel, sickness, real emergencies. Don't burn them on "didn't feel like it."
    SIX
    Protein, every meal
    130-150g per day. Without it you lose muscle, not fat. End up "skinny-fat" at 90 kg. Whey + paneer + dal + soya + curd. Non-negotiable for vegetarians.
    SEVEN
    Sunday meal prep, 90 minutes
    Soya keema, paneer base, boiled dal, washed produce. This one habit saves 30 min daily and prevents weekday food collapses.
    EIGHT
    Water, 4-5 litres daily
    Two 1L bottles you can see. Count them. Most weight loss "stalls" are actually dehydration making the scale lie.
    NINE
    Photos every 2 weeks, measurements monthly
    Scale lies. Photos and waist tape don't. Same lighting, same pose, same time of day. The week scale doesn't move but waist drops 1 inch — that's pure fat loss.
    TEN
    No liquid calories except milk
    No juice, no flavored milk, no sweet lassi, no sugar in tea/coffee. Black coffee, green tea, water, plain chaas. Milk in moderation. That's it.
    ELEVEN
    Adjust at month 3
    Your maintenance calories drop as you lose weight. Around week 10-12, expect a plateau. Drop calories by 100-150, add one cardio session. This is biology, not failure.
    What will try to break this
    Week 2-3: The body protests
    Initial enthusiasm fades. Muscles ache. Voice in your head says "one day off won't hurt." This is the make-or-break window. Push through.
    Week 4-5: The scale slows
    After initial 4-5 kg drop, weekly loss shrinks. You'll think "it's not working." It IS. Trust photos and measurements. The scale always lags behind the mirror.
    Week 6-7: The well-meaning friend
    "Yaar tu kitna patla ho gaya, ab toh khaa le ek din." They mean well. Smile, eat a little, don't make it a 3-day binge. Compliments are not permission slips.
    Week 10-12: The real plateau
    This is biology. Drop calories slightly, add one extra cardio. Don't panic, don't crash diet, don't quit. The plateau passes in 7-10 days.
    Festivals and weddings
    They will happen in 6 months. Plan to enjoy them. One meal won't undo a week. One week of binging will undo a month. Know the difference.
